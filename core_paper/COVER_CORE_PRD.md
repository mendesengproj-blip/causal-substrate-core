# Cover letter — Physical Review D

To the Editors of *Physical Review D*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"What a Lorentz-invariant
discrete order can carry: an axiomatic core for emergence on causal substrates."**

**What the paper does.** A research programme that couples matter fields to a
causal set accumulates positives (long-range order, topological solitons, an
exact meson octet, confinement) and negatives (no emergent photon, no scale, no
chirality, no spontaneous arrow of time). This paper inverts the usual question
— *does X emerge?* — and organises the record into a short deductive core:
**what is a Lorentz-invariant discrete order permitted to carry?** Two axioms (a
Poisson-sprinkled causal order, forced by the Bombelli–Henson–Sorkin theorem,
and a generic compact internal field) define an intrinsic class; one lemma fixes
what a pair of events carries (*time with sign, space without sign and without
structure*); one boundary theorem reads the isometry group through three
structural layers — non-compact orbits force divergent valence (no scale, no
photon); disconnected components cannot condense (no parity violation); the
compact part protects discrete indices (topological charge, exact multiplets).

**What is new here.** (i) The loop side of the argument is upgraded from
measurement to a **combinatorial trichotomy** — including a hinge theorem:
square-generated percolating loop structure *forces* a grading, an intrinsic
foliation, and therefore cannot coexist with boost invariance. (ii)
Renormalization is built in: Poisson is the exact fixed point and global
attractor of thinning, with explicit eigenvalues and no invariant relevant
direction. (iii) A parity no-go in strong form: $P$ acts trivially on the
intrinsic configuration space, so chirality has no carrier — explicitly or
spontaneously. (iv) The theory keeps exactly **two external inputs, each with a
proof of its own necessity**: the Skyrme-type core cost (dominance impossible at
tree level by a pointwise identity, and measured not to emerge at one loop, at
$\sim$130$\sigma$) and the single lattice-to-SI unit. (v) The forced covariant
(Sorkin–Johnston) quantization is measured to open: stable vacuum, relativistic
infrared branch, at the structural cost of one $T$-bit.

**What we are careful to claim.** Every statement is about the *class* defined
by the axioms, never about Nature; claims carry explicit epistemic grades
([theorem]/[sketch]/[measured]). The places where the principle does **not**
deliver are a section of the paper, not a footnote — including a measured
super-area matter entropy that breaks the area law, displayed rather than
absorbed. The paper closes with the ten open frontiers and the named joints at
which the theory can be attacked and broken.

**Why PRD.** The manuscript is gravitation and field theory on discrete
substrates — causal set kinematics, emergent gauge/Goldstone structure, lattice
measurements, and theorems about both — within the journal's scope. Companion
manuscripts reporting the measured inputs are under review at PRD and CQG; the
source repositories are public and linked in the references.

The manuscript is original and is not under consideration elsewhere. I declare
no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
