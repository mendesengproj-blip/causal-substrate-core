# Cover letter — Classical and Quantum Gravity

To the Editors of *Classical and Quantum Gravity*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Why Lorentz-invariant causal
sets cannot support an emergent $U(1)$: a causal complement to string-net
theory."**

**What the paper does.** Two research programmes derive gauge structure rather
than postulate it: in condensed matter, string-net condensation produces
emergent photons as collective modes of loop degrees of freedom, with sixty
years of emergence phenomenology behind it; in quantum gravity, causal set
theory replaces spacetime by a Lorentz-invariant discrete order — and no causal
substrate has ever produced an emergent photon. The paper argues these two
facts have a single explanation, and that it is a **theorem about signature**
rather than a technical gap. String-net condensation needs three structures:
finite valence, closed loops of finite dimension, and an oriented spacelike
2-cell to carry magnetic flux. A Lorentz-invariant causal substrate refuses all
three, each traceable to the non-compactness of the boost — the same
non-compactness that makes every invariant connection rule on a Poisson
sprinkling have infinite expected valence (a Campbell–Mecke computation).

**Why this is a complement, not an attack.** The paper does not dispute
string-net theory; it explains why its mechanism lives entirely on the
definite-signature side of a sharp boundary. Wick rotation is exactly the
operation that crosses that boundary (it compactifies the group), and an order
cannot cross it and remain an order. The absence of an emergent photon on
causal sets is thereby reframed: not an open problem awaiting a cleverer
construction, but the signature doing to gauge structure what it demonstrably
does to valence and to criticality. The claim is falsifiable at three named
joints, stated in the paper.

**Relation to other work.** A companion manuscript reporting the systematic
*measured* search for an emergent photon on causal sets (the polarisation-
locking exclusion, an indefinite-signature Benincasa–Dowker gauge operator, the
electric-only diamond population, and the curvature lever) has been submitted
separately to this journal; the present paper is the theoretical explanation of
that map, and each stands alone. The measured inputs are cited with their
public source repositories.

**Why CQG.** Causal set theory, Lorentz invariance of discrete structures, and
the transportability of condensed-matter emergence mechanisms to quantum-gravity
substrates are squarely within the journal's scope, and the string-net side is
presented so as to be readable by both communities.
